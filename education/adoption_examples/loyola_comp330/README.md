# Loyola University Chicago COMP330/474 — Software Engineering

## Purpose

This directory contains the flagship educational implementation of the ETIS (Engineering Trustworthy Intelligent Systems) Educational Ecosystem.

Loyola University Chicago COMP330/474 serves as both a real software engineering course and the primary educational feedback system used to continuously refine ETIS.

This implementation demonstrates how ETIS can be operationalized within a semester-long software engineering experience while preserving the relationship between educational theory and educational practice.

This directory is not the ETIS Educational Ecosystem itself.

It is the implementation record that shows how ETIS is taught, evaluated, improved, and evolved through real classroom experiences.

---

## Core Philosophy

COMP330/474 is a bi-directional educational laboratory where ETIS informs the course and the course continuously informs ETIS.

The course consumes ETIS.

The course also contributes back to ETIS.

Educational theory and educational practice continuously reinforce one another.

This relationship is intentional.

It creates a feedback system that allows ETIS to remain grounded in real educational experiences rather than abstract educational assumptions.

Educational work should resemble professional engineering work.

---

## Educational Mission

The goal is not to teach students how to complete assignments.

The goal is to teach students how to become trustworthy engineers.

Students learn to:

* define intent
* engineer context
* bound authority
* verify behavior
* operate reality
* explain decisions
* own outcomes

Students should graduate with evidence of engineering ability rather than evidence of course completion.

---

## Professional Portfolio Philosophy

Students are not building a class project.

Students are building the early stages of a professional engineering portfolio.

Students are encouraged to select projects that they may continue developing for one to two years beyond the course whenever practical.

The objective is to create evidence of engineering ability that students may later use during internships, interviews, and professional employment opportunities.

Students should graduate with demonstrable engineering experience rather than isolated academic artifacts.

---

## Educational Structure

The course typically operates as a 15-week semester.

Typical enrollment is approximately 30 students.

Students work in teams of five to six members.

Each team works on a single project throughout the semester.

The course uses the ETIS Two-Cycle Engineering Model.

Students progressively mature from implementation-focused thinking toward operational and trustworthy engineering thinking.

The implementation uses six engineering phase gates to provide continuous accountability throughout the semester.

Engineering accountability is distributed across the entire semester rather than concentrated at the end.

---

## Graduate Student Integration

Graduate students enrolled in COMP474 are intentionally distributed across teams.

Graduate students frequently serve as team leaders and help elevate engineering expectations across the teams.

The objective is not hierarchy.

The objective is mentorship, leadership, and stronger engineering maturity across the entire classroom.

Mixed undergraduate and graduate teams create richer engineering experiences.

---

## Repository-Centered Engineering

Repository-centered engineering is foundational to this implementation.

GitHub is required.

GitHub was selected because:

* it is widely adopted professionally
* students are already familiar with it
* it is freely available to students
* it supports repository-centered engineering

The course intentionally emphasizes engineering principles rather than specific tools.

Tools will evolve over time.

Engineering behaviors should endure.

Repositories become engineering memory systems.

Students learn that repositories preserve more than source code.

Repositories preserve:

* requirements
* plans
* architecture
* AI usage
* reviews
* testing evidence
* release evidence
* operational thinking

Repository-centered engineering creates continuity between education and professional practice.

---

## AI Philosophy

AI usage is encouraged throughout the entire engineering lifecycle.

Students are encouraged to use AI for:

* brainstorming
* requirements analysis
* architecture discussions
* implementation
* reviews
* testing
* operational thinking
* reflection

However, AI usage must remain governed, visible, and accountable.

The governing principle remains:

> AI proposes; engineers verify.

Students quickly learn that responsible AI usage creates significant engineering advantages.

Teams that avoid AI entirely often struggle to keep pace with teams that responsibly integrate AI into their workflows.

The objective is not AI dependence.

The objective is AI responsibility.
