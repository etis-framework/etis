# Playbooks

## Purpose

The `playbooks` directory contains educational assets that teach professional engineering behaviors, habits, expectations, and practices.

The purpose of these assets is to help learners develop the human dimensions of engineering that extend beyond technical implementation.

Software engineering is fundamentally a collaborative discipline.

Engineers must routinely communicate, coordinate, negotiate, review, explain, adapt, and support one another throughout the engineering lifecycle.

Playbooks help transform students into professionals by making these behaviors explicit rather than leaving them to be discovered through trial and error.

---

## Mission

The mission of `playbooks` is to teach engineers how to think, behave, collaborate, and contribute within modern engineering environments.

These assets help learners understand that successful engineering is not solely an individual technical activity.

It is a professional team activity that requires discipline, communication, accountability, and shared responsibility.

The objective is not to teach students how to complete assignments.

The objective is to teach students how successful engineers operate.

---

## Educational Philosophy

Many engineering failures are not caused by technical limitations.

They are caused by human limitations.

Projects frequently struggle because teams fail to:

* Communicate effectively
* Clarify expectations
* Share information
* Surface risks
* Ask questions
* Coordinate responsibilities
* Resolve conflicts
* Preserve accountability

These behaviors should be intentionally taught rather than assumed.

Playbooks make these expectations visible.

## Foundational ETIS Principles

The following ETIS principles strongly influence playbook design.

### AI proposes; engineers verify.

Engineers remain responsible for decisions and outcomes.

### Governance is architecture.

Professional behaviors establish governance throughout engineering work.

### Context is control.

Teams must actively build and maintain shared context.

### Everything important leaves evidence.

Communication and decisions should be documented when appropriate.

### The model is not the system.

Successful engineering requires understanding systems, organizations, and people.

### A demo is not operational proof.

Long-term success depends upon sustainable engineering behaviors.

Assets within this directory should help engineers learn to:

### Communicate professionally

### Collaborate intentionally

### Develop accountability

### Support teammates

Contribute to collective success.

### Build engineering discipline

Establish repeatable professional habits.

### Anticipate challenges

Recognize potential risks before they become problems.

### Develop engineering judgment

Learn how experienced engineers think and operate.

* Team operating practices

---

## What Does Not Belong Here

The following do not belong in this directory:

* Fill-in templates
* Technical reference documentation
* Repository setup instructions
* Grading rubrics
* Tool installation instructions
* Assignment specifications

Those assets belong elsewhere within the ecosystem.

Playbooks should remain focused on human behaviors.

## Professional Engineering Reality

Many students initially believe software engineering success is determined primarily by technical skill.

Professional environments quickly reveal a broader reality.

Successful engineers routinely demonstrate:

* Reliability
* Communication
* Collaboration
* Adaptability
* Accountability
* Leadership
* Judgment

These qualities often distinguish exceptional engineers from merely competent engineers.

Playbooks intentionally teach these behaviors.

## Governance Rules

The following rules apply to all assets within this directory.

### Teach durable behaviors.

### Teach professional practice.

### Teach team success.
