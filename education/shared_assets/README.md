# Shared Assets

## Purpose

The `shared_assets` directory contains reusable educational intellectual property used throughout the ETIS (Engineering Trustworthy Intelligent Systems) Educational Ecosystem.

These assets are intentionally engineered, governed, and maintained as first-class ETIS artifacts.

The purpose of this directory is to prevent educational duplication, preserve engineering memory, and enable multiple educational implementations to consume a common body of engineering practices, templates, workflows, and evaluation mechanisms.

Shared assets transform educational materials into long-lived educational infrastructure.

Educational implementations consume these assets rather than permanently owning them.

---

## Mission

The mission of `shared_assets` is to teach future engineers how to responsibly engineer, govern, review, operate, and steward intelligent systems.

These assets help learners develop professional engineering behaviors rather than simply complete assignments.

The objective is not to teach students how to build software.

The objective is to teach students how to become trustworthy engineers.

---

## Educational Philosophy

AI capability increases engineering responsibility.

As AI systems become increasingly capable, educational systems must evolve beyond teaching implementation skills alone.

Future engineers must learn to:

* Define intent
* Engineer context
* Bound authority
* Verify behavior
* Operate reality
* Explain decisions
* Own outcomes

These responsibilities are reinforced through reusable educational assets.

---

## Category Overview

### playbooks/

Playbooks teach professional behaviors, habits, expectations, and engineering practices.

Playbooks explain how successful engineers, teams, reviewers, and organizations behave.

Examples include:

* Professional communication
* Team behaviors
* Student success patterns
* Project orientation guidance

Playbooks primarily answer:

**How should engineers think and behave?**

---

### templates/

Templates are fill-in engineering artifacts that learners progressively complete.

Templates provide structured engineering scaffolding while preserving learner ownership.

Examples include:

* Team charters
* Requirements documents
* Architecture overviews
* Risk registers
* Test plans
* Release notes
* Postmortems
* Runbooks

Templates primarily answer:

**What evidence should engineers create?**

---

### workflows/

Workflows teach repository-centered engineering processes.

Workflows help students understand how engineering work moves from idea to operation.

Examples include:

* Repository setup
* Repository structure
* Traceability
* Pull requests
* Definition of Done
* Demonstration readiness

Workflows primarily answer:

**How does engineering work flow through a system?**

---

### ai_engineering/

AI Engineering assets teach responsible AI usage.

AI systems are powerful collaborators but remain bounded participants within larger engineering systems.

Examples include:

* AI usage expectations
* AI disclosure guidance
* Verification expectations
* Human accountability practices

AI Engineering primarily answers:

**How should engineers responsibly collaborate with AI?**

---

### assessment/

Assessment assets evaluate engineering maturity rather than output volume.

The goal is not to reward activity.

The goal is to evaluate professional engineering development.

Examples include:

* Engineering review rubrics
* Release readiness evaluation
* Operational readiness evaluation
* Phase gate assessments

Assessment primarily answers:

**How do we know engineering responsibility is developing?**

---

## Relationship to Educational Implementations

Educational implementations consume shared assets.

Educational implementations do not permanently own shared assets.

Current implementation:

```text

```

Future implementations may include:

* Additional universities
* Graduate programs
* Capstone experiences
* Professional workshops
* Organizational training programs

Shared assets enable these implementations to maintain a common engineering philosophy while allowing implementation-specific customization.

## Governance Principles

The following rules govern all shared assets.

### Educational architecture is intentional.

Educational ecosystems require information architecture.

### Assets have a single authoritative home.

Duplicate assets should be avoided.

### Markdown is authoritative.

Authoritative format:

`.md`

Distribution format:

`.pdf`

Optional editable format:

`.docx`

### Shared assets are reusable.

Assets should be implementation-independent whenever possible.

### Educational assets are intellectual property.

Educational artifacts are engineered products requiring stewardship.

### Engineering memory should be preserved.

Assets should evolve without losing historical context.

---

## Future Evolution

This directory is expected to grow over time.

Growth should occur within existing architectural boundaries whenever possible.

New asset categories should only be introduced if a significant architectural need emerges.

The architecture should prioritize long-term maintainability over short-term convenience.

---

## Core Thesis

Future engineers will not be judged solely by whether they can build software.

They will increasingly be judged by whether they can responsibly define intent, engineer context, bound authority, verify behavior, operate reality, explain decisions, and own outcomes.

The assets contained within this directory exist to teach those responsibilities.
