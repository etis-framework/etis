# Assessment

## Purpose

The `assessment` directory contains educational assets that evaluate engineering maturity, engineering responsibility, and professional development.

The purpose of these assets is to help learners understand that software engineering is not solely measured by whether software functions correctly.

Engineering work must also be understandable, explainable, reviewable, governable, operationally sustainable, and trustworthy.

Assessment within ETIS is intentionally broader than grading.

Assessment is a mechanism for developing professional engineers.

---

## Mission

The mission of `assessment` is to evaluate how engineers think, communicate, make decisions, exercise judgment, and assume responsibility throughout the engineering lifecycle.

Assessment should reinforce engineering behaviors that students will encounter throughout their professional careers.

The objective is not to reward activity.

The objective is to develop trustworthy engineers.

---

## Educational Philosophy

Professional engineers are continuously evaluated.

Engineering organizations routinely evaluate:

* Requirements quality
* Architectural decisions
* Risk awareness
* Implementation quality
* Testing effectiveness
* Release readiness
* Operational preparedness
* Communication
* Leadership
* Accountability

Educational environments should prepare students for these realities.

Assessment should therefore mirror professional engineering evaluation rather than traditional academic grading alone.

---

## Foundational ETIS Principles

The following ETIS principles strongly influence assessment design.

### AI proposes; engineers verify.

Evaluation should reward verification and engineering judgment.

### Governance is architecture.

Assessment should evaluate governance behaviors.

### Context is control.

Students should demonstrate the ability to define and maintain context.

### Everything important leaves evidence.

Assessment should value engineering evidence.

### The model is not the system.

Students should understand systems beyond implementation.

### A demo is not operational proof.

Assessment should evaluate long-term sustainability rather than short-term demonstrations.

## Educational Objectives

Assets within this directory should help engineers learn to:

### Think systematically

Understand relationships between engineering activities.

### Make intentional decisions

Recognize tradeoffs and assumptions.

### Communicate professionally

Explain engineering work to others.

### Defend engineering choices

Provide evidence supporting decisions.

### Anticipate operational realities

Consider sustainability beyond implementation.

### Exercise engineering judgment

Demonstrate thoughtful decision making.

### Accept accountability

Take ownership of outcomes.

## Assessment Philosophy

ETIS assessment intentionally shifts educational emphasis away from the question:

**Did the software work?**

Toward a broader set of questions.

**Was intent clearly defined?**

**Were assumptions identified?**

**Was evidence preserved?**

**Were risks considered?**

**Were decisions explainable?**

**Was engineering work reviewable?**

**Could the system survive operation?**

These questions better reflect modern engineering practice.

---

## Governance Rules

The following rules apply to all assets within this directory.

### Evaluate engineering maturity over output volume.

Quantity should not be confused with quality.

### Reward evidence.

Students should support decisions with evidence.

### Encourage explainability.

Students should explain important decisions.

### Evaluate systems thinking.

Students should understand relationships between engineering activities.

### Reinforce accountability.

Students should own outcomes.

### Mirror professional practice.

Assessment should resemble real engineering evaluation processes.
