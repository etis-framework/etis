# AI Engineering

## Purpose

The `ai_engineering` directory contains educational assets that teach responsible AI-assisted engineering practices.

The purpose of these assets is to help future engineers effectively collaborate with AI systems while preserving human accountability, engineering judgment, governance, and operational ownership.

AI systems are powerful engineering collaborators, but they are not autonomous engineers.

These assets teach students and professionals how to responsibly integrate AI into engineering workflows without transferring accountability to the technology itself.

AI participation increases engineering responsibility rather than reducing it.

---

## Mission

The mission of `ai_engineering` is to teach engineers how to responsibly use AI throughout the software engineering lifecycle.

This includes teaching engineers how to:

* Properly use AI assistance
* Verify AI outputs
* Disclose AI participation
* Bound AI authority
* Preserve engineering evidence
* Exercise human judgment
* Maintain accountability

The goal is not to maximize AI usage.

The goal is to maximize trustworthy engineering.

---

## Educational Philosophy

AI changes the nature of engineering work.

AI can generate artifacts, propose solutions, summarize information, explain code, and accelerate many engineering activities.

However, AI does not own outcomes.

Human engineers remain responsible for:

* Decisions
* Tradeoffs
* Verification
* Governance
* Operations
* Releases
* Consequences

Educational systems must intentionally teach these responsibilities.

Students should learn how to work with AI without becoming dependent upon AI.

---

## Foundational ETIS Principles

The following ETIS principles are particularly important within AI-assisted engineering.

### AI proposes; engineers verify.

AI output should never be accepted without validation.

### Governance is architecture.

AI usage should be intentionally governed.

### Context is control.

AI effectiveness depends heavily upon context quality.

### Everything important leaves evidence.

AI participation should be documented when appropriate.

### The model is not the system.

AI is a component within larger engineering systems.

### A demo is not operational proof.

AI-generated solutions must survive real-world operation.

## Educational Objectives

Assets within this directory should help engineers learn to:

### Define intent

Clearly communicate goals and desired outcomes.

### Engineer context

Provide AI systems with sufficient information and boundaries.

### Bound authority

Determine what AI may and may not do.

### Verify behavior

Validate outputs before acceptance.

### Explain decisions

Maintain human understanding of important decisions.

### Preserve evidence

Document meaningful AI participation.

### Own outcomes

Retain accountability for the final result.

## What Does Not Belong Here

The following do not belong in this directory:

* Vendor-specific marketing material
* Tool installation instructions
* AI product comparisons
* Temporary AI trends
* Tool-specific tutorials with limited longevity

The focus is intentionally durable.

The emphasis should remain on engineering principles rather than rapidly changing technologies.

## Governance Rules

The following rules apply to all assets within this directory.

### Teach principles over tools.

Tool ecosystems will evolve.

Engineering principles should remain durable.

### Preserve human accountability.

AI never owns outcomes.

### Require verification.

AI outputs should not be accepted without review.

### Preserve evidence.

Important AI participation should be documented.

### Encourage transparency.

AI usage should be appropriately disclosed.

### Maintain engineering judgment.

Human judgment remains central to trustworthy engineering.

## Core Thesis

AI will increasingly participate in engineering systems.

Future engineers will not be judged by whether they can use AI.

They will be judged by whether they can responsibly direct, govern, verify, explain, and own the outcomes produced through human-AI collaboration.
