# ETIS Classroom Facilitation

Educational Operations Engine

## Purpose

Classroom facilitation transforms educational architecture into lived educational experiences.

This directory helps instructors operationalize ETIS within the classroom environment by providing repeatable systems for orchestrating discussions, engineering exercises, reviews, AI interactions, team activities, and professional decision-making experiences.

This is not classroom management.

This is educational operations engineering.

Software engineering education is no longer solely about delivering content. It is about designing and operating environments that progressively transform students into trustworthy engineers.

Educational experiences should resemble professional engineering work.

Students should not feel like they are completing isolated academic exercises.

Students should feel like they are participating in authentic engineering systems.

Classroom facilitation operationalizes that transformation.

---

## Core Question

This directory answers:

> How do instructors actually run ETIS classroom experiences?

More specifically:

> How do instructors orchestrate educational experiences that progressively transform students into trustworthy engineers?

## Educational Operations Philosophy

ETIS classrooms are not lecture delivery systems.

ETIS classrooms are engineering ecosystems.

Instructors are not information distributors.

Instructors are educational operators.

Their responsibility is to create environments where students progressively learn to:

- define intent
- engineer context
- bound authority
- verify behavior
- operate reality
- explain decisions
- own outcomes

Educational experiences should mirror professional engineering experiences.

Educational maturity should mirror engineering maturity.

Student responsibility should progressively increase over time.

The classroom should become less instructor-centered and more engineering-centered as the course matures.

## Facilitation Principles

ETIS classroom experiences should be:

### Intentional

Every activity should exist for a reason.

Students should understand why activities occur.

### Authentic

Educational work should resemble professional engineering work.

Artificial academic exercises should be minimized.

### Evidence Driven

Students should produce artifacts.

Artifacts should create evidence.

Evidence should support conclusions.

### Review Oriented

Engineering work is not complete until it can be reviewed.

Review should become a normal engineering behavior.

### AI Responsible

AI usage is expected.

AI dependency must be disclosed.

AI outputs must be verified.

AI responsibility should be taught continuously rather than isolated to individual assignments.

### Operational

Students should increasingly think about systems after implementation.

Operational thinking should become habitual.

## Constitutional Rules

Preserve these principles.

- Educational work should resemble professional engineering work.
- Engineering accountability is the educational outcome, not the side effect.
- Engineering work is not complete until it can be defended.
- AI usage is not an academic violation. Undisclosed and unverified AI dependency is an engineering risk.
- Do not assess AI avoidance. Assess AI responsibility.
- Evidence maturity should mirror engineering maturity.
- Assessment maturity should mirror engineering maturity.
- Scale complexity, not accountability.
- Scale evaluation mechanisms, not engineering expectations.
