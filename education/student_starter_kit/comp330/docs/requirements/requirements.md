# Requirements

## Purpose

This document preserves the project requirements that define what the system is expected to do and why those expectations matter.

Requirements are engineering evidence.

They should help another reviewer understand:

* what problem the team is solving
* who the system serves
* what capabilities are expected
* what constraints exist
* what assumptions remain unresolved
* how requirements connect to acceptance criteria, design, testing, and release readiness

## Requirement Format

Use this format for each requirement.

```text
Requirement ID:
Title:
Description:
Rationale:
Source:
Priority:
Status:
Related Acceptance Criteria:
Related Tests:
Related Risks:
```

## Requirement Review Questions

Before considering requirements ready, answer:

* Are the requirements understandable?
* Are ambiguous terms identified?
* Are assumptions documented?
* Are acceptance criteria defined?
* Are requirements testable?
* Are risks visible?
* Are AI-assisted requirement changes disclosed and verified?

## Engineering Standard

A requirement is not complete because it is written.

A requirement is complete when another engineer can understand it, review it, test it, and trace it to evidence.
