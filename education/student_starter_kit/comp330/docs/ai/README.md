# AI Governance Package

## Purpose

This directory helps teams govern AI responsibly throughout the project.

AI should be treated as an engineering capability rather than an engineering replacement.

The goal is not avoiding AI.

The goal is responsibly using AI while maintaining human ownership.

AI may accelerate work.

AI does not transfer responsibility.

Students remain responsible for:

- decisions
- risks
- verification
- outcomes

Keep AI governance lightweight and practical.

Do not over-engineer it.

---

## AI Artifacts

| Artifact | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| `ai-policy.md` | Defines team AI usage expectations |
| `ai-use-log.md` | Records meaningful AI usage |
| `ai-verification-notes.md` | Records how AI outputs were verified |

---

## Relationship To ETIS Philosophy

AI proposes; engineers verify.

Students should continuously ask:

- What AI was used?
- What was delegated?
- What was verified?
- What remains human owned?
- What risks remain?

AI governance should become routine.

AI should not feel exceptional.

AI should become normal engineering practice.

---

## AI Progression

This directory intentionally supports the following progression.

```text
AI Boundaries

↓

AI Usage

↓

AI Verification
```

Strong engineering teams govern AI intentionally.

---

## Review Questions

Teams should periodically ask:

- Is AI usage visible?
- Are AI outputs being verified?
- Are risks being surfaced?
- Is ownership remaining human?

---

## Engineering Standard

Delegation is not ownership.

Ownership never transfers to AI.
