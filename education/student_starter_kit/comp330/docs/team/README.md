# Team Package

## Purpose

This directory helps transform a group of students into an engineering team.

Engineering teams are engineered, not assembled.

The goal is not simply dividing work.

The goal is learning how engineers collaborate to build trustworthy systems.

This directory provides lightweight artifacts that help teams establish:

- shared purpose
- visible ownership
- healthy working norms
- consistent communication

These artifacts should remain lightweight and evolve throughout the project.

Do not over-engineer them.

The purpose is improving teamwork, not creating additional paperwork.

---

## Team Artifacts

| Artifact | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| `team-charter.md` | Defines the team's mission, goals, and commitments |
| `roles.md` | Defines ownership and responsibilities |
| `working-agreements.md` | Defines how the team will operate together |
| `communication-plan.md` | Defines how important information moves through the team |

---

## Relationship To The ETIS Philosophy

Educational work should resemble professional engineering work.

Professional engineering teams intentionally define:

- why they exist,
- who owns what,
- how they operate,
- and how they communicate.

Student teams should practice these same behaviors.

---

## Team Progression

This directory intentionally supports the following progression.

```text
Team Identity

↓

Team Ownership

↓

Team Norms

↓

Team Communication
```

Strong engineering organizations develop intentionally over time.

---

## Review Questions

Teams should periodically ask:

- Is ownership clear?
- Are blockers being surfaced early?
- Is communication effective?
- Are team agreements helping or hurting?
- Are responsibilities balanced?

---

## Engineering Standard

Healthy engineering teams make ownership, expectations, and communication visible.
