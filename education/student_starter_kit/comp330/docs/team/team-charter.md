# Team Charter

## Purpose

This document defines the team's mission, goals, and engineering commitments.

The charter should be lightweight and revisited throughout the project.

The charter is not a contract.

It is a shared understanding of why the team exists.

## Team Mission

Describe the engineering problem your team is trying to solve.

Example:

> Build a trustworthy, reviewable, and maintainable system that solves a meaningful problem while practicing responsible engineering behaviors.

---

## Team Goals

List 3-5 goals.

Examples:

- Build a defensible solution.
- Create engineering evidence continuously.
- Practice responsible AI usage.
- Maintain a sustainable team workflow.

---

## Success Criteria

How will the team define success?

Examples:

- Working software
- Strong documentation
- Visible engineering evidence
- Positive team collaboration
- Defensible engineering decisions

---

## Team Commitments

We commit to:

- review engineering work
- communicate openly
- surface risks early
- continuously improve
- own outcomes

---

## Review Questions

Review periodically.

- Is our mission still accurate?
- Are our goals still realistic?
- Are we operating according to our commitments?

---

## Engineering Standard

Strong engineering teams intentionally define how they will operate before they begin building.
