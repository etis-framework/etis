# Reviews Package

## Purpose

This directory helps teams challenge engineering work before claiming success.

Reviews are not inspections performed at the end of the project.

Reviews are continuous engineering activities.

Reviews improve quality.

Reviews reduce risk.

Reviews increase trust.

Strong engineering teams review work continuously.

Keep reviews lightweight and practical.

Do not over-engineer them.

---

## Review Artifacts

| Artifact | Purpose |
|----------|---------|
| `architecture-review.md` | Reviews architectural decisions and system structure |
| `code-review.md` | Reviews implementation quality and maintainability |
| `ai-review.md` | Reviews AI-assisted engineering work |
| `release-readiness-review.md` | Reviews whether engineering claims are defensible |

---

## Relationship To ETIS Philosophy

Engineering work is not complete until it can be defended.

Reviews make engineering work defensible.

Students should continuously ask:

- What assumptions are being made?
- What risks remain?
- What evidence exists?
- What still needs verification?

---

## Review Progression

This directory intentionally supports the following progression.

```text
Architecture Review

↓

Code Review

↓

AI Review

↓

Release Review
```

Strong engineering teams challenge work continuously.

---

## Review Questions

Teams should periodically ask:

- Are reviews occurring regularly?
- Are risks being surfaced?
- Are assumptions being challenged?
- Are reviews improving the project?

---

## Engineering Standard

Strong engineering teams make review a habit rather than an event.
